Motivation

Segmented liver organs from computed tomography (CT) scans are critical for radiation therapy planning, image-guided surgery, hepatic disease diagnois and functionality assessment. Manual delineation of the liver from hundreds of CT slices per scan is labor and time consuming. Traditional computer vision and image processing methods are not robust given the variations in image quality of CT (spatial resolutions, signal-to-noise ratio) and human anatomy, and can take a significant amount of time (in minutes) and sometimes require user interactive to provide additional input.


A deep learning model taught to learn from the variations in CT image quality and human anatomy by training on a large dataset size. The segmentation predictions are also fully automated, fast (in seconds) and require no user intervention.

Solution

A 3D U-Net based deep learning model was developed and trained with segmented liver organs from CTs from the Liver Tumor Segmentation Challenge (LiTS) . A different and unseen dataset from 3Dircadb was used for independent validation. High dice and Hausdorff 95 scores were observed for the predicted liver segmentations.
